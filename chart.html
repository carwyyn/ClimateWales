What is Wales' contribution?

Next year, Welsh Ministers will announce for the first time legal carbon limits for the period up to 2050 to reduce emissions in Wales by 80%.

On the eve of this historic event, this investigation looks at the challenges ahead, analyses the different emission categories, and investigates how the government is doing on previously set targets.

Wales' historic industrial history has had an impact on our natural environment, and the remnants can still be seen in our emissions today.

The lates figures, for 2015, show that in 2015 Wales emmitted 46 mega tonnes of greenhouse gas carbon dioxide equivalents (MtCO2e). Thus, Wales makes up 9.1% of UK emissions, and 1.1% of European Union emissions. However, at a emissions per capita outlook, Wales constantly appears near the top of the table of OECD reporting countries.
